Hosting for podcasts, radio programs

Teal is a audio and feed host with a program-episode relationship. It automatically produces an iTunes feed for podcasting and lets you keeps track of content played. It allows direct uploads or broadcast stream capture.

iTunes feed creation

For every program, Teal automatically creates an iTunes feed to publish your program as a podcast on iTunes.

Passwordless login

Teal sends an email with a special login link for logins. No passwords = no passwords to forget.

API-first

The provided frontend uses the same API that is available to you. You can build your own tools to talk to Teal or automate everything. Scroll below for docs.

Record from a stream

Teal allows you to record live programs from a remote online stream. This way you can turn your radio station broadcast into neatly cataloged programs and episodes.

Show notes/markdown support

Teal shows your show notes correctly on iTunes streams with markdown support.

No lock-in

Teal hosts and helps you manage audio. Online services shoud empower you, not lock you in. Teal allows you to forward requests to your feed somewhere else if you change your mind after publishing.

Unlimited programs and episodes

You can create unlimited programs and episodes.

You control everything

Teal can host audio files and your iTunes feed. You can also choose to do one of those yourself and create a hybrid solution. Teal gives you the ability to point where your audio file is for your feed.

Log songs

Teal allows you to keep track of the songs on your episode. You can also subscribe to the logs live using a pub/sub model if recording from a stream at the same time. This is handy if you have a broadcast stream and want to associate tracks with the time it played on the stream.

API Documentation

Authentication

Every account is authenticated over email:

curl -X POST -d '{"email":"<YOUR EMAIL>"}' http://api.teal.cool/login

or using the shortcut version:

curl -X POST http://api.teal.cool/login?email=<YOUR EMAIL>

The login method doesn't return any useful information. Once an email is sent, it will expire in 8 minutes. During this time, you can not request an other authentication email.

When you open your email account you will see an email from login@teal.cool. At this point, you can choose to also login using the frontend. If you do, your key will be accessible at api.teal.cool/key from your browser. Note that every time you request a key, the old one will be invalidated.

You can also choose only to get your key directly:

curl http://api.teal.cool/auth?token=aZ3vNUb0Rjm7gBK4BeBjgg

API keys will never expire but every successful login will generate a new one.
